Camel

  1. There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels
  2. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes
  3. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.

Chameleons

  • Chameleons have eyes in the backs of their heads
  • Chameleons come in a lot of different shapes and sizes
  • Chameleons have extremely powerful tongues

Pigeons

  1. Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals
  2. Pigeons are highly sociable animals
  3. Both female and male pigeons share responsibility of caring for and raising young

Teddy Bear

  • The term bear-hug was first recorded in 1846
  • The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906
  • In 1902 in Germany, Steiff launched “Bear 55 PB”, the first toy bear with jointed arms and legs

Panda

  1. They spend a lot of their day eating
  2. They have great camouflage for their environment
  3. Their eyes are different to normal bears

Frog

  • One gram of the toxin produced by the skin of the golden poison dart frog could kill 100,000 people.
  • There is a frog in Indonesia that has no lungs – it breathes entirely through its skin.

Cicada

  • All cicada species in North America came from a common ancestor.
  • Occasionally, a cicada species switches from being on a 13-year cycle to a 17-year cycle
  • The cicadas are a superfamily, the Cicadoidea, of insects in the order Hemiptera.

Dogs
